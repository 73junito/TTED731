Course Description: Facilitating adult learning. Areas of concentration are motivation, adult
learners' characteristics, cultural issues, styles and patterns. Special attention will be focused on
the adult learners in occupational, correctional, basic adult education, community colleges,
extensions and proprietary student learner's programs. Focused toward identification of learning
styles, needs and how to match delivery techniques to learners needs.

Course Objectives:
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
1. Differentiate between pedagogical and andragogical approaches
2. Analyze the characteristics of adult learners
3. Design instructional strategies that promote motivation, engagement, persistence, and
retention
4. Identify barriers and challenges faced by adult learners
5. Apply principles of cognitive processing, metacognition, Universal Design for Learning
(UDL), and differentiated instruction
6. Evaluate instructional practices and learner needs across specialized adult education
environments
7. Select and justify appropriate instructional delivery
8. Create a comprehensive adult learning instructional
